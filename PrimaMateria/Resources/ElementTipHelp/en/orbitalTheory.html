Orbital Theory

Index

The quantum theory of matter that combines Schrodinger's wave mechanics and Heisenburg's uncertainty principle and applies this to the behavior and nature of electrons. Orbital theory was formulated in 1926 and has yielded a better understanding of electrons and their critical role in chemical bonding than is possible with Newtonian mechanics.

The primary orbits are numbered from 1 to 7 from the nucleus outwards. Secondary orbits are given the letters s, p, d, f (derived respectively from the words sharp, principle, diffuse and fundamental, used to describe the lines in the spectrum corresponding to electrons moving in and out of the orbits).
